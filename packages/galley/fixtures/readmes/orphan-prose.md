# orphan-prose

Dense prose whose paragraphs end on awkward word counts, to stress the widow and
orphan gate. Each paragraph should end without a single stranded short word on
its own last line.

The typographic term for a lone word left on the final line of a paragraph is a
widow, and it is one of the small ugly failures that a rendering surface is
supposed to prevent automatically without anyone having to hand-tune the copy at
all, ever, under any register or measure whatsoever.

Balancing the rag is the job of the layer, not the author. A reader should never
have to notice that the last line carried exactly one orphaned syllable across an
otherwise unremarkable and perfectly ordinary block of running body text here.
